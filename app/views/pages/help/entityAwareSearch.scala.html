We are currently testing support for entity-tagged text in Peripleo (text where references to places and people are tagged with Linked Data identifiers). Thanks to the networked nature of Peripleo's data model, this allows us to introduce an exciting new feature: Entity-aware search.

Note: entity aware search is currently an experimentation stage. We are still working on a full implementation. Expect the occasional hickup - and bear with us!

What is Entity-Aware Search?

First of all, literature search in Peripleo is fulltext-capable. That means that you can search inside the actual text content of the item, not just in the metadata (title, blurb etc.); and that the search results that are returned can include the text snippets where your search matched the content. So far for the conventional part.

Thanks to the entity tags, however, search results in Peripleo also reflect the places (and people) that occur in the vicinity of your search. Since you won't usually find this functionality in other applications, let's look at an example.

Try a search for crocodiles. We will also apply an item type filter, so that we narrow down our results to objects . In our example, the search returns a single hit - "The Histories" by Herodotus. The map shows a number of dots and regions.

We mentioned before that because of Peripleo's networked data model, a single result does not necesarily correspond to a single place. A literary work like "The Histories" is a prime example, as it contains references to hundreds of places.

What you see on the map, however, isn't all places mentioned in "The Histories" either. It's only a small part of them. Yes, you guessed it: it's those places that appear in the text in the vicinity of the term crocodiles.

Text Snippets

Click on of the markers (or regions) on the map. The item details will show one or more text snippets, relevant to the combination of the term crocodiles and the place you selected.

Source Links

If supported by the datasource, the snippets will include links that lead back to the corresponding section in the source environment. In case of our example above, the source of the text is Pelagios Commons' annotation environment Recogito which allows us to jump, for example, right into a section where crocodiles are mentioned in the context of Ionia.

Search by Metadata

Entity-aware search also covers the case where your match is based on item metadata rather than the content. Try a search for herodotus.

You will notice that the map is now much more densely populated. That's because your results include Herodotus' "The Histories" as a whole, with all the places it mentions.

Click a map marker. Again, Peripleo will show text snippets relevant to the selected place. Just without additional contextualization from a query term.
