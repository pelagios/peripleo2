Data in Peripleo comes from many different sources. Very often, these sources publish information about the same real-world thing. A major historical place like Rome for example, does, obviously, not just appear in one or two of our partners' datasets - it appears in many.

What is Linked Data

Luckily, our partners publish their data as Linked Data. Linked Data is a way of connecting online resources that have some element in common. It uses typed links (i.e. links with a meaning attached to them) to declare how different pieces of data relate to one another; or when they correspond to the same real-world thing.

Peripleo uses these links to connect equivalent records from different sources. Therefore, when you select a place in Peripleo, the information that you see - image, names, description , dates - is usually not something published by a specific single authority. Instead, it is an aggregate of information, published collectively by partners of the Pelagios Commons community.
