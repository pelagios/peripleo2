Data in Peripleo comes from many different sources. Very often, these sources publish information about the same real-world thing. A major historical place like Rome for example, does, obviously, not just appear in one or two of our partners' datasets - it appears in many.

What is Linked Data

Luckily, our partners publish their data as Linked Data. Linked Data is a way of connecting online resources that have some element in common. It uses typed links (i.e. links with a meaning attached to them) to declare how different pieces of data relate to one another; or when they correspond to the same real-world thing.

Peripleo uses these links to connect equivalent records from different sources. Therefore, when you select a place in Peripleo, the information that you see - image, names, description , dates - is usually not something published by a specific single authority. Instead, it is an aggregate of information, published collectively by partners of the Pelagios Commons community.

Looking Underneath the Hood

Peripleo's info boxes provide, by intention, only a compact overview. Ready to dive deeper into the actual Linked Data that underpins them? Enter the Linked data View.

Let's start by selecting the place Lepcis Magna. The row of coloured codes at the bottom of the info box are a hint to the Linked Data origins of the information you are looking at. Each code is the identifier for Lepcis Magna in one of Pelagios' partners' datasets. Let's take a closer look.

Click the  icon next to the row of coloured IDs. This opens the Linked Data View. The view consists of the link graph on the left side, and the record details on the right side.

The Link Graph

The link graph shows how different partners' records for Lepcis Magna connect to each other. For example the record in the Digital Atlas of the Roman Empire dare:21127 links to its counterpart in the Pleiades Gazetteer of the Ancient World pleiades:344448; Archaeological Atlas of Antiquity Vici.org vici:2448 links to to Pleiades as well as to the equivalent Wikidata record.

Exact vs. Close Match

In terms of indicating equivalence, Pelagios partners use two different types of links. (The types are defined through a Linked Data vocabulary called the Simple Knowledge Organization System SKOS.) One of the types indicates an exact match (solid line), meaning the record is conceptually identical - it represents the same place in terms of geographical, cultural and temporal scope. The other link indicates a close match (dashed line), which means the records are pretty close, but do not necessarily the exact same thing. Our partners use this type, among other things, to connect an ancient place in their dataset to its modern successor in another.

Indexed vs. External Records

Coloured dots in the graph represent records that Peripleo has indexed. White dots, on the other hand, are links to external datasets. Partners link to them in their records, but Peripleo does currently not harvest any further information from them.
